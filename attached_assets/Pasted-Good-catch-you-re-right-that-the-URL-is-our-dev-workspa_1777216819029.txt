Why dev workspace as staging: in Replit's architecture, all deployments of a single Repl share one Helium production database. So a "second deployment" labelled staging would actually write to our live production DB — your synthetic work_authorisations row, the dedup row, and the breach row would land in real customer data alongside genuine signed-off devis. Our dev workspace runtime, by contrast, is auto-injected with a separate dev Helium connection string, so synthetic events stay fully isolated. Cleaner blast radius for the test.

Staying warm during the window: Reserved VM doesn't extend to the workspace runtime — different mechanism. We're keeping the workspace warm two ways: (1) we've enabled the workspace-level Always-On setting so the runtime stays up regardless of editor activity, and (2) Clive will have the workspace open in an active browser tab from 13:50 to 14:35 CEST as belt-and-braces.
