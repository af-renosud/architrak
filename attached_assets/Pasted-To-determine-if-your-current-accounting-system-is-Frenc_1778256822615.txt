To determine if your current accounting system is "French-compatible," you need to verify if it can handle a **cumulative, three-party verification loop** rather than a simple linear "Quote → Invoice" flow.

Here is a summary of the French private construction financial workflow designed for a systems audit:

---

### 1. The Legal Foundation: Le Marché Travaux

The system must be able to anchor all future transactions to a **Marché** (Contract).

* **The Difference:** In standard accounting, a "Quote" is often just a draft invoice. In France, the *Marché* is a legal "ceiling."
* **System Requirement:** Can your system lock an initial budget and track any changes specifically as **Avenants** (Amendments) rather than just editing the original quote?

---

### 2. The Adjustment Logic: Plus-Value (PV) & Moins-Value (MV)

Your system must allow for granular adjustments to the contract value.

* **The Workflow:** If a client changes a tile choice, the system shouldn't just change the price; it needs to record a **PV** (Increase) or **MV** (Decrease).
* **System Requirement:** Does your software support "Change Orders" that maintain an audit trail of the *Original Budget* vs. the *Current Revised Budget*?

---

### 3. The Billing Mode: Situations de Travaux (Progress Billing)

This is where most standard accounting systems fail. French construction uses **cumulative** billing.

* **The Logic:** Invoice #3 is not for "30% of the work." It is for **"75% of total work done since the start, MINUS what was paid in Invoice #1 and #2."**
* **System Requirement:** Can your system track **Percentage of Completion (% Avancement)** per line item? If it only handles "Quantity" or "Flat Fees," it will struggle with French construction.

---

### 4. The Validation Filter: Certificat de Paiement

In France, the contractor’s invoice is legally just a "proposal" until the Architect signs off.

* **The Logic:** The Architect reviews the *Situation* and may "prorate" or reject certain percentages. This results in a **Certificat de Paiement**.
* **System Requirement:** Does your system allow for an **Approval Workflow** where the "Amount to Pay" can be different from the "Invoiced Amount" without creating a messy "Partial Payment" status?

---

### 5. The Legal Withholding: Retenue de Garantie (5%)

French law allows the client to withhold 5% of every invoice for one year to cover potential defects.

* **The Workflow:** On a €10,000 invoice, the client pays €9,500. The remaining €500 is tracked in a separate "bucket" called the *Retenue de Garantie*.
* **System Requirement:** Can your system automatically calculate a **5% Retention** and keep it on the balance sheet as a "Liability" or "Future Payable" rather than just showing the invoice as "Unpaid"?

---

### Summary Checklist for your System:

| Feature | Necessary for French Construction? |
| --- | --- |
| **Cumulative Invoicing** | **Yes** (Must track total work to date) |
| **Line-Item % Progress** | **Yes** (Essential for Situations) |
| **Avenant (PV/MV) Tracking** | **Yes** (For budget history) |
| **5% Retention Logic** | **Yes** (Legal requirement) |
| **Three-Party Approval** | **Yes** (Architect / Contractor / Client) |
